King James Version

23

The Divine Shepherd

A Psalm of David.

1 The Lord is my shepherd, I shall not want;
2 he makes me lie down in green pastures. He leads me beside still waters;
3 he restores my soul. He leads me in paths of righteousness for his name’s sake.
4 Even though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I fear no evil; for thou art with me; thy rod and thy staff, they comfort me.
5 Thou preparest a table before me in the presence of my enemies; thou anointest my head with oil, my cup overflows.
6 Surely goodness and mercy shall follow me all the days of my life; and I shall dwell in the house of the Lord for ever.

About

Charlemagne: An Intellectual King

The great Carolingian Emperor, known as Charles the Great – or Charlemagne – was not only known for being a conquering hero and the Father of Europe; he was also a lover of learning.

It is said that he had the Psalter and the writings of the Early Church Fathers read to him at mealtimes. Thus, the memory of this learned king was this web app's inspiration.
